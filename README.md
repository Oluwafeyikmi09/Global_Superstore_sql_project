# Global Superstore Sales Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Global Superstore dataset using SQL to uncover insights into sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, product performance, and regional trends.

The objective of this project is to demonstrate practical SQL skills used by Data Analysts to answer real business questions and support data-driven decision-making.

Through this analysis, I explored key performance indicators, customer purchasing patterns, product profitability, sales trends, and operational efficiency using SQL queries ranging from basic aggregations to advanced window functions.

---

The Global Superstore dataset contains transactional sales records from a multinational retail company.

---

## Key Insights

* A small percentage of customers contributed a significant portion of total sales revenue.
* Certain products generated strong sales but delivered low profitability due to high discount levels.
* Some sub-categories consistently underperformed and recorded negative profits.
* Regional performance varied significantly, indicating opportunities for targeted business strategies.
* Sales showed seasonal fluctuations, highlighting peak and low-demand periods.

---

## Recommendations

### Improve Discount Strategy

Review discount policies on products with consistently low profit margins to protect profitability.

### Focus on High-Performing Products

Increase inventory and marketing efforts for products that generate both strong sales and healthy profits.

### Retain High-Value Customers

Develop customer retention programs for top-spending customers to maximize lifetime value.

### Address Loss-Making Products

Investigate products with recurring negative profits and consider pricing adjustments or discontinuation.

### Regional Optimization

Allocate resources and marketing investments toward regions with strong profitability while addressing underperforming markets.
